What Does an Online IQ Test Actually Measure?

Traditional IQ tests like the Wechsler or Stanford-Binet are administered by psychologists and take several hours. Online IQ tests are a faster proxy — they measure the same cognitive building blocks but in a condensed format.

A well-designed online IQ test covers:

  • Pattern Recognition — identifying rules in sequences, matrices, and visual grids
  • Logical Reasoning — deducing conclusions from premises, syllogisms, odd-one-out
  • Working Memory — holding and manipulating information in short-term memory
  • Verbal Reasoning — analogies, word relationships, vocabulary in context
  • Mental Agility — processing speed, reaction time, quick calculation
  • Spatial Awareness — mental rotation, grid puzzles, visual patterns
  • Numerical Reasoning — number sequences, mental math, quantitative logic
  • Problem Solving — multi-step reasoning, code-breaking, deductive puzzles

What Is a Good IQ Score?

IQ scores follow a normal distribution with a mean of 100 and a standard deviation of 15. Here's what different score ranges mean:

  • Below 70 — Intellectual disability range
  • 70–84 — Below average
  • 85–99 — Low average to average
  • 100–114 — Average to high average (most people score here)
  • 115–129 — Above average / bright
  • 130–144 — Gifted
  • 145+ — Highly gifted / genius range

The average IQ is 100 by definition. Scoring above 115 puts you in the top 16% of the population. Above 130 is the top 2%. Don't stress about the number — what matters more is whether your score is improving over time.

Can You Improve Your IQ Score?

This is one of the most debated questions in cognitive science. The consensus: your baseline fluid intelligence is largely genetic, but your performance on IQ-style tasks absolutely improves with practice.

Specifically, research shows that training on:

  • Working memory tasks (like N-back) improves fluid intelligence markers
  • Pattern recognition practice increases speed and accuracy on novel problems
  • Mental math training significantly improves numerical processing speed
  • Regular cognitive challenges slow age-related cognitive decline

Whether that constitutes a "real" IQ increase depends on your definition. But if your goal is to perform better on IQ tests and think more sharply in everyday life, consistent brain training works. See our full guide: How to Increase Your IQ Score.
