Mental Math Tricks That Make You Calculate Instantly

Mental arithmetic isn't just a party trick — it's a genuine cognitive workout. The process of calculating in your head forces your working memory to hold multiple pieces of information simultaneously while performing operations, directly training the brain systems measured by IQ tests.

Here are the most powerful mental math shortcuts, organized by operation type.

Multiplication Tricks

Multiply Any Number by 11

For two-digit numbers: 43 × 11 = 4_(4+3)_3 = 473. Add the two digits together and place the sum in the middle. If the sum exceeds 9 (e.g., 75 × 11 = 7_(7+5)_5 = 7_12_5), carry the 1: 825.

The 5× Shortcut

Multiply by 5 = multiply by 10 and halve. 68 × 5: 68 × 10 = 680, ÷2 = 340. Works instantly for any number and is much faster than traditional multiplication.

Squares Near 50

For numbers near 50: (50+n)² = 2500 + 100n + n². So 52² = 2500 + 200 + 4 = 2704. 47² = 2500 - 300 + 9 = 2209. Once memorized, this covers a useful range without calculation.

Multiply Two Numbers Near 100

96 × 97: Find how far each is from 100 (4 and 3). Subtract either deficit from the other number: 96-3=93 or 97-4=93. Multiply the deficits: 4×3=12. Answer: 9312. Works for any numbers close to 100.

Addition & Subtraction Tricks

Round and Adjust

347 + 289: Round 289 to 290, add: 347+290=637, subtract 1: 636. Or round 347 to 350, add 289 = 639, subtract 3: 636. Rounding to the nearest 10 makes mental addition trivial.

Left-to-Right Addition

Traditional addition goes right-to-left (units first). Mental math works better left-to-right. 456 + 278: 400+200=600, 50+70=120 (total 720), 6+8=14 (total 734). Each step is simple and the running total is always in range.

Division Tricks

Divide by 5 Instantly

Divide by 5 = multiply by 2 and divide by 10. 840 ÷ 5: 840 × 2 = 1680, ÷10 = 168. Always faster than direct division.

Divisibility Rules

Quickly check if a number is divisible: by 3 (sum of digits divisible by 3), by 4 (last two digits divisible by 4), by 6 (divisible by both 2 and 3), by 9 (sum of digits divisible by 9). These eliminate most division before you start.

Percentage Shortcuts

The Commutative Percentage Trick

x% of y = y% of x. So 7% of 50 = 50% of 7 = 3.5. Always choose whichever direction is easier. 8% of 25 = 25% of 8 = 2. This simple trick makes most percentage problems trivial.

10% as a Building Block

Find 10% first (move decimal one place left), then build from there. 35% of 80: 10%=8, 30%=24, 5%=4, total=28. 15% tip on $64: 10%=$6.40, 5%=$3.20, total=$9.60. Nearly all percentages reduce to combinations of 10%, 5%, and 1%.

Why Mental Math Improves Your IQ Score

Mental calculation is a direct workout for working memory — you hold intermediate results while performing operations. Regular mental math practice also improves number sense, pattern recognition in numerical data, and processing speed. All of these contribute to higher scores on the numerical reasoning and mental agility components of IQ tests.
